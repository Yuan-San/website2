New Technology Experience : Carbon Countermeasures

Propagation of Dryobalanops Aromatica/Sumatran Camphor Tree through Stem Cutting and Tissue Culture for Forestation of Sumatra and Kalimantan.

Fostering Carbon Emissions Reduction, Conserving Endangered Species Trees, Building Long-term Local Economy, and Improving Socioeconomic Development and Livelihoods for Indonesians.

 Global Warming and Climate Change

Earth's temperature increase of 2°C compared to the temperature in pre-industrial times is associated with severe negative impacts on the natural environment and human health, which also affect the climate and made the phenomenon of Climate Change. 

A much higher risk that is very dangerous and possibly causing catastrophic changes around the globe.  Climate change causes shifts, These shifts cause frequent and intense drought, storms, heat waves, rising sea levels, melting glaciers, and warming oceans.

Countering Climate Change

The largest carbon dioxide emissions arise from deforestation. Indonesia’s high release of greenhouse gasses is mainly due to Forests losing their trees. Because of that,  Indonesia planned to reduce its carbon emission by 41 percent by the year 2030.

However many global efforts on reforestation failed due to saplings not
getting enough rainfall, planted in poor habitats, and also being planted
at the wrong time.
It is calculated up to 90 percent of the saplings from the world initiative of

Step 3

S  O  L  U  T  I  O  N    P  R  O  P  O  S  A  L

Finding the Right Native Trees

Dryobalanops Aromatica is the right tree for Propagation and Reforestation because of its many useful contributions such as Carbon Reduction, Building the Local Economy, and Improving Socioeconomic Development in Indonesia. 

The Dryobalanops aromatica, known as the Borneo Camphor, has been grown in natural forests, since the 7th century and became an International trade icon at that time. It is native to the Sumatra and Borneo islands and was originally found grown in forests. 

Due to large exploitation and illegal logging, the existence of Dryobalanops in Indonesia’s natural forest is increasingly threatened. The IUCN has categorized D. sumatrenesis as VULNERABLE since 2018. In-situ and ex-situ conservation efforts must be made to maintain the existence of the species.

Building Local Economy

Camphor woods are known to be highly resistant to termites. This makes the material more durable for furniture and housing.\

Camphor trees' Branches and Leaves produce a soothing fragrance. They can be turned into essential oils.

Borneol, a product of the camphor tree is a core element in herbal medicine. They can help with digestion and circulation.

Step 4

S  T  A  R  T    O  F    T  H  E    R  E  S  E  A  R  C  H    I  N  V  E  S  T  I  G  A  T  I  O  N

The Beginning of Research Investigation started on February 11th, 2021, The school gracefully received 2 Dryobalanops aromatica trees from the Silvi culture department, Faculty of Forestry, Bogor Agricultural University (IPB).

Initial Trial

Propagation by stem cutting commenced on February 2021, the media that were used were sand, rice husk media, and a combination of both media.

However, the propagation experiment failed because, at the time, the explants that were used were too young to develop any calluses.

Step 5

O  N  G  O  I  N  G    R  E  S  E  A  R  C  H    I  N  V  E  S  T  I  G  A  T  I  O  N

1. Stem Cutting

A year after receiving the Dryobalanops aromatica plants, it has grown to over 3 meters long and the explants were mature enough to be able to develop calluses. We decided to attempt propagation via stem cutting a second time. Now with 4 variations of media and several hormones for growth. 

Media that is Replicated are Sand, Swamp, Dirt, and Cocopeat. Hormones used are IBA, Rootone, and Gibberellic Acid.

Plants that are Stem-cut are maintained and conducted inside the incubator of the Greenhouse laboratory of Technonatura.

2. Vitro Stem Cutting

Vegetative propagation by stem cutting, conducted inside aseptic tissue culture materials at the Plant Tissue Culture Laboratory of TechnoNatura.

The media used in this experiment are Murashige Skoog (MS) and Woody Plant Medium (WPM). While Hormones variations that are used are IBA, BAP, and Gibberellic Acid.

Sterilization Methods

1. 70% EtOH 3min, 10% Clorox 10min

2. 70% EtOH 3sec, 10% Clorox 10min, 0.3% HgCl 5min

3. 70% EtOH 3min, 10% Clorox 10min, 0.3% HgCl 5min

4. 70% EtOH 3min, 10% Clorox 10min, 0.3% HgCl 7min

3. Cloning

Plants are cloned to produce identical plants quickly and economically. Producing new plants by cloning is quicker than allowing plants to reproduce than collecting and sowing seeds. It's therefore an effective way of producing new individuals from rare and endangered plants, helping to preserve the species. 

Sterilizations happen In-situ and at Lab. Mediums that are used are Murashige Skoog (MS), Woody Plant Medium (WPM), and Gamborg. We also use Sitokinin, Auxin, Gibberellic Acid, and BAP for the cloning' Hormones.

We feel challenged and happy to initiate and perform this research and study to develop new technologies in combatting the climate change by making our research project to study the biotechnological cloning of endangered trees to be used for reforestation of the Sumatra and Kalimantan (Borneo).
